# Best Audio in 2026

I spent weeks testing 5 different audio. Some were great, some were terrible, and most were somewhere in between.

Here's what I actually recommend, based on real usage—not affiliate commissions.

## My Top Picks


### 1. ElevenLabs — Best Overall

- **Price:** $5-99/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.5/5
- **Best for:** Voiceovers, Audiobooks

AI voice generation and cloning platform.

**Pros:** Most realistic voices; Voice cloning
**Cons:** Can be expensive; Ethical concerns

### 2. Suno — Best Value

- **Price:** $10/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.3/5
- **Best for:** Music creation, Background music

AI music generation tool that creates complete songs from text.

**Pros:** Impressive quality; Easy to use
**Cons:** Limited control; Copyright concerns

### 3. Murf — Best for Voiceovers

- **Price:** $26/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.1/5
- **Best for:** Voiceovers, E-learning

AI voiceover platform for creating professional voice content.

**Pros:** Professional voices; Easy to use
**Cons:** Expensive; Limited free tier

### 4. Play.ht — Best for Podcasts

- **Price:** $31.20/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.0/5
- **Best for:** Podcasts, Audiobooks

AI voice generation platform with ultra-realistic voices.

**Pros:** Very realistic voices; Good for podcasts
**Cons:** Expensive; Complex pricing

### 5. Speechify — Best for Reading assistance

- **Price:** $139/year
- **Rating:** ★ 4.0/5
- **Best for:** Reading assistance, Accessibility

Text-to-speech app for reading articles, documents, and books.

**Pros:** Great for reading; Mobile app
**Cons:** Expensive yearly; Quality varies


## How I Tested

I used each tool for at least a week on real projects. I looked at:

- **Ease of use:** Can you figure it out in 10 minutes?
- **Value for money:** Is it worth the price?
- **Reliability:** Does it work consistently?
- **Support:** What happens when things go wrong?

## What to Look For

When choosing a audio, consider:

1. **Your budget** — Prices range from free to hundreds per month
2. **Your use case** — Different tools excel at different things
3. **Integration** — Does it work with your existing tools?
4. **Learning curve** — How much time will you spend learning it?

## The Bottom Line

There's no single "best" audio. It depends on your needs. But if I had to pick one, I'd go with **ElevenLabs**. It's not perfect, but it's the most well-rounded option I've tested.
