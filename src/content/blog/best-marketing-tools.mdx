# Best Marketing in 2026

I spent weeks testing 7 different marketing. Some were great, some were terrible, and most were somewhere in between.

Here's what I actually recommend, based on real usage—not affiliate commissions.

## My Top Picks


### 1. Ahrefs — Best Overall

- **Price:** $99/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.6/5
- **Best for:** SEO, Link building

SEO toolset for link building, keyword research, and competitor analysis.

**Pros:** Best backlink data; Great for SEO
**Cons:** Expensive; No free trial

### 2. SEMrush — Best Value

- **Price:** $129.95/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.5/5
- **Best for:** SEO, Content marketing

Comprehensive SEO and digital marketing platform.

**Pros:** Most comprehensive; Great data
**Cons:** Expensive; Overwhelming

### 3. Surfer SEO — Best for Content optimization

- **Price:** $49-199/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.4/5
- **Best for:** Content optimization, SEO strategy

AI-powered SEO content optimization tool.

**Pros:** Best content optimization; Data-driven
**Cons:** Expensive; Can be overwhelming

### 4. Intercom — Best for Customer support

- **Price:** $74/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.3/5
- **Best for:** Customer support, Sales

Customer messaging platform with AI chatbot.

**Pros:** Comprehensive; Good AI
**Cons:** Expensive; Complex

### 5. Mailchimp — Best for Email marketing

- **Price:** $13/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.2/5
- **Best for:** Email marketing, Newsletters

Email marketing platform with AI-powered features.

**Pros:** Easy to use; Good templates
**Cons:** Gets expensive; Limited automation free

### 6. Zendesk — Best for Customer support

- **Price:** $19/mo
- **Rating:** ★ 4.2/5
- **Best for:** Customer support, Help desk

Customer service platform with AI-powered support.

**Pros:** Comprehensive; Scalable
**Cons:** Can be expensive; Complex setup

### 7. Drift — Best for Sales qualification

- **Price:** Custom
- **Rating:** ★ 4.1/5
- **Best for:** Sales qualification, Lead routing

Revenue acceleration platform with conversational AI.

**Pros:** Good for sales; AI qualification
**Cons:** Very expensive; Complex


## How I Tested

I used each tool for at least a week on real projects. I looked at:

- **Ease of use:** Can you figure it out in 10 minutes?
- **Value for money:** Is it worth the price?
- **Reliability:** Does it work consistently?
- **Support:** What happens when things go wrong?

## What to Look For

When choosing a marketing, consider:

1. **Your budget** — Prices range from free to hundreds per month
2. **Your use case** — Different tools excel at different things
3. **Integration** — Does it work with your existing tools?
4. **Learning curve** — How much time will you spend learning it?

## The Bottom Line

There's no single "best" marketing. It depends on your needs. But if I had to pick one, I'd go with **Ahrefs**. It's not perfect, but it's the most well-rounded option I've tested.
