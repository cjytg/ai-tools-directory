## Features Deep Dive

Let's break down what Logseq actually offers and how each feature performs in practice.

### Bi-directional links

The bi-directional linking system automatically creates connections between related notes. When you link to another note, a backlink is created automatically, building a web of interconnected knowledge that reveals relationships you might not have noticed.

### Graph view

The graph view visualizes all your notes and their connections as an interactive network. This provides a bird's-eye view of your knowledge base, revealing clusters of related topics and isolated ideas that could benefit from more connections.

### Outliner

The outliner interface organizes notes as nested bullet points, making it easy to break down complex topics into hierarchical structures. You can expand or collapse sections to focus on specific levels of detail.

### Local first

The local-first architecture stores all your notes on your device by default. Your data never leaves your computer unless you explicitly choose to sync it, giving you complete ownership and control over your knowledge base.
