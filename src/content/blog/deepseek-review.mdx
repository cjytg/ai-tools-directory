# DeepSeek Review 2026: Is This Free AI Chatbot Better Than GPT-4?

I've been testing DeepSeek against ChatGPT and Claude for a few weeks, and here's my honest take. This isn't a sponsored post—I found it on my own and decided to put it through its paces.

**Quick verdict:** DeepSeek delivers impressive coding and math performance for free. At Free, it's absolutely worth trying if you're a developer or student.

## What Is DeepSeek?

DeepSeek is made by DeepSeek, founded in 2023. It's designed for developers, students, researchers, and it shows. The tool focuses on coding, math problem solving, research.

## Key Features

- **Strong coding ability**
- **Math reasoning**
- **Open weights**
- **Generous free tier**

The coding ability is genuinely competitive with GPT-4 in my testing. It handles code generation, debugging, and refactoring well. The math reasoning is also surprisingly strong for a free tool.

## Pricing

- **Price:** Free
- **Free tier:** Yes
- **Rating:** ★ 4.3/5

Completely free to use. There's no catch—the open weights model means anyone can run it locally or use their hosted version without paying.

## What I Like (Pros)

- Impressive free performance
- Great for coding
- Open model availability

## What I Don't Like (Cons)

- Less polished UX
- Slower response times
- Limited ecosystem

To be fair, the UX is where you feel the "free" label most. The interface is functional but not beautiful, and response times can lag during peak hours.

## Who Should Use DeepSeek?

If you're a developers looking for coding assistance, DeepSeek is worth a look. It's particularly good for math problem solving.

**Skip it if:** You need a polished, feature-rich experience with integrations. Check out ChatGPT, Claude, Mistral instead.

## Alternatives

Speaking of alternatives, here's how DeepSeek compares:

ChatGPT, Claude, Mistral are all options worth considering. ChatGPT has the broadest ecosystem, Claude excels at document analysis, and Mistral offers good European data privacy.

## The Bottom Line

DeepSeek delivers on its promises. It's not perfect—the UX could be better—but overall, I'd recommend it as a free alternative for coding and research tasks.

**Final rating: 4.3/5**
