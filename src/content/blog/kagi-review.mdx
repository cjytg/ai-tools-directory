# Kagi Review 2026: Is Premium Search Worth Paying For?

I've been using Kagi as my daily search engine for months now, and here's my honest take. This isn't a sponsored post—I pay for it with my own money.

**Quick verdict:** Kagi delivers ad-free, high-quality search results with useful AI summaries. At $5-10/mo, it's worth considering if you search a lot.

## What Is Kagi?

Kagi is made by Kagi, founded in 2021. It's designed for privacy-conscious users, researchers, professionals, and it shows. The tool focuses on privacy-focused search, research, daily use.

## Key Features

- **Ad-free search**
- **AI summaries**
- **Customizable rankings**
- **Privacy-focused**

The ad-free experience is the biggest selling point. No ads, no tracking, no SEO spam clogging up your results. The AI summaries are genuinely useful for research queries—they pull key facts from top sources so you don't have to click through multiple pages.

## Pricing

- **Price:** $5-10/mo
- **Free tier:** No
- **Rating:** ★ 4.4/5

Kagi doesn't have a free tier—you pay $5/mo for the Starter plan or $10/mo for Professional. It's the only premium search engine that I know of that's gained real traction.

## What I Like (Pros)

- No ads or tracking
- High-quality results
- AI-powered summaries

## What I Don't Like (Cons)

- Paid only
- Smaller index than Google
- Less mainstream

To be fair, the smaller index means you'll occasionally get fewer results for obscure queries. But for common searches, the quality more than makes up for it.

## Who Should Use Kagi?

If you're a privacy-conscious users looking for privacy-focused search, Kagi is worth a look. It's particularly good for research.

**Skip it if:** You need the broadest possible search index or can't justify paying for search. Check out Perplexity AI, Google Gemini, DuckDuckGo instead.

## Alternatives

Speaking of alternatives, here's how Kagi compares:

Perplexity AI, Google Gemini, DuckDuckGo are all options worth considering. Perplexity AI has better AI summaries, Google Gemini has the largest index, and DuckDuckGo is free and privacy-focused.

## The Bottom Line

Kagi delivers on its promises. It's not perfect—the paid-only model won't appeal to everyone—but overall, I'd recommend it for anyone who values their time and privacy online.

**Final rating: 4.4/5**
