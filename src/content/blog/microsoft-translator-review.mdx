# Microsoft Translator Review 2026: Is It Worth It?

I've been using Microsoft Translator for a while now, and here's my honest take. This isn't a sponsored post—I bought the subscription myself and tested it thoroughly.

**Quick verdict:** Microsoft Translator is microsoft's translation service with offline capabilities. At Free, it's decent but has better alternatives.

## What Is Microsoft Translator?

Microsoft Translator is made by Microsoft, founded in 2015. It's designed for business users, office workers, travelers, and it shows. The tool focuses on business translation, office documents, offline use, microsoft ecosystem.

Let me break down what you actually get.

## Key Features

- **70+ languages**
- **Offline mode**
- **Office integration**
- **API**

The features are solid. I particularly like the 70+ languages—it works well and doesn't feel gimmicky.

## Pricing

Here's where it gets interesting:

- **Price:** Free
- **Free tier:** Yes, completely free
- **Rating:** ★ 3.9/5

This is completely free, which is rare.

## What I Like (Pros)

- Free
- Offline mode
- Office integration

## What I Don't Like (Cons)

- Fewer languages than Google
- Less accurate
- Smaller community

To be fair, some of these cons are minor. But I promised an honest review, so there you go.

## Who Should Use Microsoft Translator?

If you're a business users looking for business translation, Microsoft Translator is worth a look. It's particularly good for office documents.

**Skip it if:** You're on a tight budget or need something more powerful. Check out DeepL, Google Translate instead.

## Alternatives

Speaking of alternatives, here's how Microsoft Translator compares:

DeepL, Google Translate are all options worth considering. Each has its own strengths.

## The Bottom Line

Microsoft Translator is decent but not amazing. It's not perfect—the fewer languages than google is annoying—but overall, there are better options out there.

**Final rating: 3.9/5**
